Why Temperature and Humidity?

Food Waste

14% of food produced for human consumption is lost between harvest and retail, according to the Food and Agriculture Organization. This may not look as much in percentage but this represents 1.3 billion tons of food every year.

Temperature

Improper temperature control is one of the main causes of premature food spoilage, especially during storage and transportation. Most food items are highly sensitive to temperature fluctuations.

Humidity

Maintaining the correct humidity level in storage environments is essential for preserving food quality. Too much moisture can lead to mold and spoilage, while too little can cause dehydration and loss of freshness.

Real-Time Monitoring

Real-time monitoring of temperature and humidity in storage facilities is crucial to prevent spoilage. Using sensors to ensure that any fluctuations are immediately addressed, helping preserve food quality and reduce waste.
